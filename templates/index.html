Are We Living the ACA Dream?

Examining health disparities between race, urban v rural, and 4 specific healthcare measures.

Health policy writers have a duty to keep all populations in mind when writing new laws.


Overview

The bar charts below show the average for each particular variable, either by state or national level. The dropdown allows for toggling to individual states or 'all' to see the average nationally.

By understanding 1) the average mortality of these health measures, 2) the percentage of the population that is rural and urban, 3) and the percentage of the population that is white compared to black/African-American, we can then begin to look at how changes in these values, when cross-analyzed, might highlight concerning outcomes in these specific communities.


State:

These histograms compare the frequency of county mortality rates in the U.S., for the selected health measure. How the groups are separated: The mean race(white and black) population of each county in the U.S. was taken. Then the aggregate mean of all the counties was taken. Those counties equal or above the mean of each race was then placed into their respective groups. The groups, white and black, are compared based on taking the mean population of each race in each county in the U.S of each race
